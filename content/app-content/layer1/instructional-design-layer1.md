## Page 1 — What Is Instructional Design?
**Page Type:** A (Overview)  
**Sensitivity:** Low  
**Depth:** Overview

### Purpose
This page introduces the field of instructional design and its role in creating effective learning experiences.

### Overview
Instructional design is the systematic process of creating educational experiences that make the acquisition of knowledge and skills more effective and efficient.

### Core Activities
Instructional designers:
- analyze learning needs
- design learning objectives
- develop instructional materials
- implement and evaluate solutions

### Scope of This Module
This module covers foundational instructional design models and their practical application.

## Page 2 — ADDIE Model
**Page Type:** B (Concept)  
**Sensitivity:** Low  
**Depth:** Overview

### Summary
- ADDIE is a widely-used instructional design framework.
- It consists of five phases: Analysis, Design, Development, Implementation, Evaluation.
- The model provides structure for systematic course development.

### The Five Phases
- **Analysis:** Identify learning needs and goals.
- **Design:** Plan learning objectives and assessment strategies.
- **Development:** Create instructional materials.
- **Implementation:** Deliver the instruction.
- **Evaluation:** Assess effectiveness and make improvements.

### Why ADDIE Matters
ADDIE provides a systematic approach that ensures alignment between goals, content, and assessment.

## Page 3 — Learning Objectives
**Page Type:** B (Concept)  
**Sensitivity:** Low  
**Depth:** Overview

### Summary
- Learning objectives define what learners should know or be able to do.
- Well-written objectives guide instruction and assessment.
- Objectives should be specific, measurable, and achievable.

### Characteristics of Effective Objectives
- **Specific:** Clearly state the expected outcome.
- **Measurable:** Include criteria for assessment.
- **Achievable:** Appropriate for learner level.
- **Relevant:** Aligned with course goals.

### Application in Design
Learning objectives inform:
- content selection and sequencing
- instructional strategies
- assessment design
