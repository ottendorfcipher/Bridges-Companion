## Page 1 — Introduction to Digital Literacy
**Page Type:** A (Overview)  
**Sensitivity:** Low  
**Depth:** Overview

### Purpose
This page introduces digital literacy as a fundamental 21st-century skill.

### Overview
Digital literacy encompasses the knowledge, skills, and behaviors needed to effectively navigate, evaluate, and create information using digital technologies.

### Core Components
Digital literacy includes:
- technical proficiency with digital tools
- information evaluation and verification
- digital communication skills
- online safety and digital citizenship

### Scope of This Module
This module covers essential digital literacy skills for learners and educators.

## Page 2 — Information Evaluation
**Page Type:** B (Concept)  
**Sensitivity:** Medium  
**Depth:** Overview

### Summary
- Evaluating online information is a critical digital skill.
- Not all information online is accurate or reliable.
- Specific strategies help assess credibility.

### Evaluation Strategies
- **Source Analysis:** Consider the author and publisher.
- **Evidence Check:** Look for supporting data and citations.
- **Cross-Reference:** Verify information across multiple sources.
- **Bias Awareness:** Recognize potential motivations and perspectives.

### Why This Matters
Information literacy supports informed decision-making and critical thinking in digital environments.

## Page 3 — Digital Citizenship
**Page Type:** B (Concept)  
**Sensitivity:** Low  
**Depth:** Overview

### Summary
- Digital citizenship refers to responsible technology use.
- It includes ethical behavior, respect, and safety online.
- Digital citizens contribute positively to online communities.

### Key Principles
- **Respect:** Treat others with courtesy online.
- **Privacy:** Protect personal information.
- **Integrity:** Practice honesty and give credit to sources.
- **Safety:** Recognize and avoid online risks.

### Educational Application
Teaching digital citizenship prepares students for responsible participation in digital society.
