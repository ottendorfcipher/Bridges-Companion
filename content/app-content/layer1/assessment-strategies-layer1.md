## Page 1 — Types of Assessment
**Page Type:** A (Overview)  
**Sensitivity:** Low  
**Depth:** Overview

### Purpose
This page introduces different types of educational assessment and their purposes.

### Overview
Assessment is the systematic process of gathering and analyzing information about student learning to inform instruction and support growth.

### Major Assessment Categories
- **Formative Assessment:** Ongoing feedback during learning.
- **Summative Assessment:** Evaluation of learning outcomes.
- **Diagnostic Assessment:** Identification of learning needs.
- **Performance Assessment:** Demonstration of skills in authentic contexts.

### Scope of This Module
This module explores assessment types, strategies, and best practices for measuring and supporting learning.

## Page 2 — Formative Assessment
**Page Type:** B (Concept)  
**Sensitivity:** Low  
**Depth:** Overview

### Summary
- Formative assessment occurs during the learning process.
- It provides feedback to guide instruction and learning.
- Formative approaches support continuous improvement.

### Common Formative Strategies
- **Exit Tickets:** Brief end-of-lesson reflections.
- **Questioning:** Strategic use of questions to check understanding.
- **Observation:** Monitoring student work and engagement.
- **Self-Assessment:** Students evaluate their own progress.

### Why Formative Assessment Matters
Timely feedback allows teachers and students to adjust strategies before summative evaluation.

## Page 3 — Authentic Assessment
**Page Type:** B (Concept)  
**Sensitivity:** Low  
**Depth:** Overview

### Summary
- Authentic assessment measures learning through real-world tasks.
- It emphasizes application over recall.
- Authentic tasks promote deeper engagement.

### Examples of Authentic Assessment
- Projects that solve real problems
- Presentations to authentic audiences
- Portfolios showcasing growth over time
- Performance tasks in realistic contexts

### Benefits
- Greater student engagement
- Transfer to real-world situations
- Development of higher-order thinking skills
